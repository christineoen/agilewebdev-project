Summary

Christine Oen is a full-stack software engineer with a career spanning tax technology consulting, nearly a decade of product engineering, and ongoing graduate study in information technology. She brings a rare combination of technical depth, commercial awareness, and cross-industry experience to every project she works on.

Christine began her career at Deloitte Tax LLP in Dallas, where she spent five years as a Senior Tax Consultant partnering with multinational corporations across oil and gas, technology, and manufacturing. She delivered tax technology solutions, trained clients nationally and internationally, supervised junior consultants, and developed Excel-based automation tools — an early sign of her instinct to solve repetitive problems with code.

In 2014 she made a deliberate pivot into software, completing an accelerated program at MakerSquare before joining NetSuite and TNO as a Technical Consultant. There she developed custom SuiteScript API implementations, customised field validations, led business requirement sessions, and performed data migration for SuiteCommerce Advanced implementations.

From October 2015 to early 2025, Christine served as a Software Engineer at One More Cloud, Inc. in Austin, Texas. Over nearly a decade she built and maintained Ruby on Rails applications with JavaScript and React frontends, automated server provisioning across AWS and Google Cloud Platform, integrated third-party APIs for billing and sales workflows, and collaborated with Platform and UX teams to deliver Elasticsearch-powered search solutions. She acted as the final code reviewer on a team of up to six engineers and authored technical documentation for customers and internal administrators.

Throughout her engineering career Christine has embraced AI-assisted development workflows — working with tools such as GitHub Copilot and Claude to accelerate delivery while maintaining rigorous code quality standards. She is currently completing a Master of Information Technology at the University of Western Australia, and her personal projects include Prorate Mate, a React and Vite application that visualises proration charges across multiple billing periods.

Her background in both accounting and engineering gives her an uncommon ability to bridge technical and business contexts — communicating clearly with stakeholders at every level and delivering solutions that are both well-crafted and commercially grounded.
